This is the
Redpath Museum Open-Source Project

This project is a student initiative lead by theMcGill Additive Manufacturing Student Society (MAMSS) and the Redpath Museum, in collaboration with students from ÉTS and Université de Montréal.

This project aims to enhance museum experiences by leveraging cutting 3D technologies and the IoT.

The Details

Our Goals

We aim to virtualize all artifacts in the Redpath Museum with only volunteers and students from the participating universities. This way we want to give visitors the ability to interact with artifacts, deliver more information on the displayed artifacts and give access to the undisplayed ones.


To achieve this ambitious objective, we created a production line for which we continously improve all 3 main components: 3D scanning artifacts, Virtual Exhibit App and 3D Printing artifacts.

3D Scanning

To 3D scan our objects, we chose to use photogrammetry as it is the most versatile and cheapest way to of 3D scanning artifacts. Briefly, 3D scanning consists of taking photos of an object from all angles and stitching those photos to create a virtual replica.


We are currently doing the whole process manually, but simultaneously are developing an automate. Here is a test from our first prototype.

Virtual Exhibit App

We developed a web application that displays our 3D scans along with 3D annotations and an accompanied article containing texts, images and videos. Click here to see it in action


To start the app, visitors must walk close to a bluetooth beacon to get introductory videos and information about the collection which they are visiting. Then, the visitors must scan a QR code that is on te artifact so that he can see both physical and virtual artifact simultaneously.


Our app was designed to enable the creation of completely different content for the same virtual artifact. This way we hope to be able to reach a wider audience no matter their age, expertise or spoken language. Our content creators, with the help from students in REDM 400, continously create and improve our content.


We just launched the first version of our app and are currently developping the next version which will include interactive educational games and the ability for visitors to order 3D prints of their favorite artifact and receive it on the same day.

3D printing

We are continously creating 3D printable versions of our 3D scans to create educational tools and souvenirs. We optimized our 3D prints so that they can be printed in 1 hour or less. We are now testing new materials and adding mechanical features to our 3D scans with the help of MAMSS's 3D printing experts and participants in their Quickfire competition.
